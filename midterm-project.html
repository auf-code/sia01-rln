1. Project Overview

In this project, you will analyze a real-world systems problem and produce a formal Systems Architecture and Systems Integration document. The focus is not on building the system, but on thinking like a systems architect: defining system boundaries, identifying interacting subsystems, modeling business processes, and justifying architectural and integration decisions. This project reflects how systems are designed and documented in real organizations before development begins.

2. Project Objectives

  • Analyze a real-world problem from a systems perspective
  • Design a high-level system architecture
  • Model business processes and system interactions
  • Identify and justify integration mechanisms
  • Communicate design decisions using standard diagrams and technical documentation

3. Project Scope & Constraints

Required Characteristics

  • Involve multiple interacting subsystems
  • Include at least one external system or service
  • Have a clear end-to-end business process
  • Require data exchange or integration

Not Allowed

  • Simple CRUD-only applications
  • Single-user or single-component systems
  • Pure UI or mobile-only applications
  • Systems without integration points

8. Non-Functional Considerations

Purpose: Address quality attributes like scalability, performance, and reliability.
Sample Content: Scalability: The system can scale vertically by increasing server resources during peak enrollment periods.
Performance: Caching frequently accessed course data reduces database load.
Reliability: Timeout and retry mechanisms are applied when communicating with external payment services.

9. Risks & Trade-offs

Purpose: Demonstrate architectural judgment and awareness of limitations.
Sample Content: A key risk is the dependency on an external payment gateway, which may experience downtime. This risk is mitigated by implementing retry logic and clear error messaging. Choosing a monolithic architecture simplifies development but limits independent scaling of components, which may require refactoring in the future.
